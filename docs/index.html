Eyeo 2015 // Robotic Poetry

I'm generally ambivalent to robo-gibberish; in a way it's the default, the easiest thing you can get a machine to spit out. All the same, when it comes to the creative, LLMs these days seem to have either been over-trained or are genuinely incapable of minting something creatively new (despite being themselves entirely new tech).

Even with the most explicit coaxing, OpenAI's monster will only generate rather bland, trite passages and will absolutely fixate on the request for "poetry." This produces embarrasingly infantile texts that resemble the stereotype of the word with zero substance. To its detriment, it is also never pretty.

So, then, I can see the artistic appeal of randomness or the sponataneity of a well-selected corpus of words to run Markov chains on. "AI," be it if statements or black-box, is however still a tool. To a great degree I've found that it is too easy to merely present the tool itself instead of meaningfully executing a vision through it.

I know I myself am guilty of this as well, using randomness in place of merely trying out options myself. The amusement comes, like it does in Allison's work, from the serendipity of the odd amusing combination.

Is that a good thing? Is that art? Maybe. Though, as the human agency in art diminishes - so does its value, as far as I can tell. ~
